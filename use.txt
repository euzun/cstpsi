Intended use and limitations
============================

INTENDED USE
  A research prototype and reproducibility artifact for Composable Set-Threshold
  PSI (CSTPSI). It demonstrates the protocol end to end, lets reviewers run it on
  their own inputs, and regenerates the paper's head-to-head performance table.
  It is meant for evaluation and research, not production deployment. It is
  licensed for noncommercial use (PolyForm Noncommercial License 1.0.0);
  commercial use requires a separate license from the author.

THREAT MODEL
  Two-party, semi-honest (honest-but-curious). Both parties follow the protocol;
  security holds against passive corruption of either party. There is no defense
  against malicious deviation, and no protection for metadata such as set sizes,
  which are assumed public.

OPERATING RANGE / LIMITATIONS
  - The construction targets small-to-moderate labels (a few identifiers).
    Per-session communication grows linearly with label length, so very large
    ("bulk") payloads are impractical.
  - The matching threshold is fixed at k = 2 of N = 64 components in this build.
  - The 1M-record cells are memory- and time-heavy; they are off by default in
    the reproducibility script (enable with FULL=1).
  - Timing is hardware-dependent. The reproducible claims are the speedup ratio
    of CSTPSI over the baseline, the communication saving, and the zero
    false-reject / zero false-accept (at T=2) correctness, not absolute latency.
  - Networking uses the loopback interface by default; the demo and benchmarks
    simulate a fast link and are not hardened for adversarial networks.

DATA
  All datasets used by the demo and the reproducibility claim are synthetic and
  generated locally by experiments/datagen.py. No real or sensitive data is
  included or required.
